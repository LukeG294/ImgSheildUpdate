The Kingdom of Mali

Luke Graham and Ryleigh Longaker

Geography

The Kingdom of Mali was located in Western Africa on the upper Niger river in the 13th to 16th century. It was named Kangaba at the time.

Mostly flat, plateaus, rivers including the Niger and Senegal. The northern part has a climate like a hot desert, and all of Mali receives little rain. The Niger river is a source of drinking water, food, and transportation. To the North, the Sahara desert is one of the hottest parts of this regation. 65% of the total land in Mali is desert.

The Niger river flooded the land and provided fertile land for agriculture, but the climate was still dangerously hot. There were desert sandstorms and many droughts.

Politics

The Empire was founded in 1235 CE by the legendary King Sundiata.

The Empire’s most famous ruler was named Mansa Musa, and chroniclers of the times wrote that when he travelled to Mecca on a pilgrimage he distributed so much gold that he caused great inflation lasting a decade.

Religion

Musa the ruler thought that Islam was an entry into the cultured world of the Eastern Medditeranian. And many people followed after him and practiced Islam.

Musa was a devout Muslim, and his pilgrimage to Mecca made him well known across northern Africa and the Middle East. They practiced there religion with pilgrimage and the traditional way just like how the Muslims practied Islam.

Religion in Mali is mostly composed of Islam, with 95 percent of the population being Muslim. The remaining 5 percent of Malians follow African religions such as the Dogon religion or Christianity. Atheism is rare among Malians.

Technology

The kingdom of Mali used natural resources such as gold to trade and pay taxes along with salt and cotton cloth. They used the Niger river for food and water.

Mathematics, and counting in particular were contributed to over 35,000 years ago in Mali.
