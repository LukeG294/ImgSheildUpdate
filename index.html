The Kingdom of Mali

Luke Graham and Ryleigh Longaker

Geography

The Kingdom of Mali was located in Western Africa on the upper Niger river in the 13th to 16th century. It was named Kangaba at the time.

Mostly flat, plateaus, rivers including the Niger and Senegal. The northern part has a climate like a hot desert, and all of Mali receives little rain. The Niger river is a source of drinking water, food, and transportation. To the North, the Sahara desert is one of the hottest parts of this regation. 65% of the total land in Mali is desert.

The Niger river flooded the land and provided fertile land for agriculture, but the climate was still dangerously hot. There were desert sandstorms and many droughts.

Technology

The kingdom of Mali used natural resources such as gold to trade and pay taxes along with salt and cotton cloth. They used the Niger river for food and water.

Chronology

Mathematics, and counting in particular were contributed to over 35,000 years ago in Mali.
